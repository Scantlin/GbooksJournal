The Frozen Frame of a Filipino Bravery

March 5, 2026

Situation or Experiences

This is the day where in we need to present our Tableau or Frozen Scene activity about the themes in classical literature. Ang pinili ng grupo namin ay ang tema ng Bravery vs. Cowardice but we also want to incorparate the other themes as well to our scene. Ang naging grupo ko ay sina Ericka, Joebhert, Laurente, Taraya at Capitan. Bawat isa sa amin ay nag-isip kung paano namin maipapakita ang temang ito sa isang static scene. From planning the concept hanggang sa execution mismo ng tableau, we gave our best. We wanted to show the bravery of the Filipino people fighting for justice against a government that is not serving the country well. We explained it clearly: the leader in our scene, more concerned with money than his duty, also represented the "Sin" of greed and the cowardice of selfishness. Meanwhile, the people deciding to fight embodied "Virtue" and "Charity"—not just fighting for themselves, but helping the country and waking others up to the truth. Those who chose to ignore the situation represented selfishness. Nagulat kami sa galing ng ibang groups, lalo na nila Dave—sobrang angas at talagang pinag-isipan ang mga eksena. We were just thankful na kahit kami, na-execute namin nang maayos ang aming scene at naintindihan naman ng audience ang mensahe namin.

Emotional and Cognitive Response

Before our presentation, medyo kaba at pressure ang naramdaman ko. Ang bigat kasi ng tema, it's about our country and the fight for what is right. We wanted to do it justice. But instead of letting the pressure get to us, we focused on our plan. We visualized how we would stand frozen, what our facial expressions should convey, and how we would explain the symbolism of bravery and cowardice in our scene. After we presented, sobrang gaan ng pakiramdam ko. We felt relief and pride because we were able to clearly show our message about fighting for justice, even in a silent, frozen scene. I also really enjoyed watching the other groups; you could see the effort they put into portraying their themes. We also appreciate how Ms. Julia critique our Tableau sobrang ganda and gagaling daw ng lahat, nakita niya ang effort ng lahat at kami nakita rin namin ang effort ng bawat isa, kahit na medyo kulang sa time since may preparation pa for TNT ay talaga namang hinasayan at binigyang pansin ni Ms.

SEL Insight

This situation highlights Social Awareness and Responsible Decision-Making. We had to be socially aware to choose a theme that was not just from a book, but relevant to what is happening in our society today. We wanted to show the reality of corruption and the people's cry for true service. In terms of responsible decision-making, we carefully planned how to depict these complex ideas in a single, powerful frozen scene. We made sure that our body language, positions, and expressions clearly communicated the contrast between the corrupt leader and the brave citizens. We decided as a group to use our voices not through dialogue, but through the strength of our silent storytelling. Actually, we are thinking about whether we should continue with the Filipino Bravery content, since some of the group are leaning toward a Classical vibe, but we push to reflect the classical theme into the modern realities.

Personal Reflection

This activity taught me that a strong message doesn't always need words. A single, well-thought-out image, like our frozen scene, can speak volumes about bravery and the fight for what is right. We proved that you don't need elaborate props or lines to show the contrast between a selfish leader and a people united for justice. I am so thankful for my groupmates who gave their best to portray this important theme. Seeing the effort of Dave and the other groups also inspired me to push my own creativity further. This experience reinforced in me the importance of using our voices, even in a silent performance to reflect on the world around us and to stand for the values we believe in, like bravery, justice, and charity.

Wandering, Fighting, and Writing as the Villain

April 06, 2026 - April 30, 2026

Situation or Experiences

This term feels very different from the prelims and midterms. Back then, we had fun, hands-on activities like roleplays and tableau, which made learning feel more like play. But this term, everything is more serious and focused on written work. We have been diving deep into lessons about three major literary periods: the Enlightenment, Romanticism, and Realism. To explain them briefly, the Enlightenment was all about reason, logic, and science, believing that progress comes from rational thinking. Romanticism then came as a reaction to that, celebrating emotion, imagination, nature, and individual feeling. Realism followed by trying to show life exactly as it is, often focusing on ordinary people, social issues, and detailed everyday struggles. Despite the heavier workload, I had a bright moment on April 23 when we had our first quiz, and I got a perfect score. I studied the three periods on the evening of April 22, but honestly, I did not review that much. Still, I am very thankful to Miss Julia because she gave us choices in the identification part, which really helped. Beyond the quiz, what stayed with me most was our lesson on Romanticism, especially the poem by William Wordsworth called “I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud,” and later the realism novel Germinal by Emile Zola. I also enjoyed the group presentations, particularly Dave’s group presenting The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn and Justin’s group presenting Gulliver’s Travels, plus the activity where we had to write a diary entry as a character from one of the stories. I chose Pap Finn, and in my diary, I wrote him as an abusive father who eventually regrets everything he did to Huck. That was my own creative choice, not something from the original book.

Emotional and Cognitive Response

I felt genuinely happy and a bit proud when I saw my perfect score on the quiz, because it showed that even a little study can pay off. At the same time, I felt grateful toward Miss Julia for making the identification part less stressful with the choices, which made me realize that small teaching decisions can really affect how a student performs. What I thought about the most, though, was the feeling of connection I had with Romanticism. When we watched and read Wordsworth’s poem, I truly related to the speaker who starts out lonely and detached but then finds peace and joy just by looking at a field of daffodils. That idea of nature healing your emotions stuck with me because I have felt that same kind of quiet happiness when I am outside or alone with my thoughts. On the other hand, Germinal by Emile Zola made me feel something different, a mix of anger and determination. Seeing how one miner stood up for what they deserved reminded me of our own situation in the Philippines, and I could not help but think that we should also fight for our rights and not let a bad government take advantage of us. (Taraa na mag rally ayyy~ HAHAHA) but seriously, we need to fight back against corruption. Also, I really enjoyed the diary activity where I chose Pap Finn from The Adventures of Huckleberry Finn. Writing as him helped me understand his character on a deeper level, especially his abuse, talagang pinagtanggol ko po yung character niya HAHAHA but I also want to choose Huck and then I will write na gusto niya talaga si Jim, kaya niya siya tinulungan, yung diary is served as confession letter kay Jim, ayy~ grabee namann kinakabog si Mark Twain HAHAH but anyway maganda talaga ang friendship nila Jim, sana sila na lang🥺👉👈.

SEL Insight

Social and Emotional Learning teaches us to recognize our emotions, understand others, and make responsible decisions, and this lesson helped me grow in that area. First, from Romanticism, I learned that it is okay to feel lonely or detached sometimes, but nature and imagination can turn that loneliness into what Wordsworth called "the bliss of solitude." Instead of fighting sad feelings, I can accept them and look for small, beautiful things around me to shift my mood. Second, from Realism and Germinal, I learned that empathy and action can go together. Seeing the miners fight for justice made me realize that when something is unfair, staying silent is not the answer. I connected that to being a Filipino, that we should not let corrupt leaders take away our future, and even small actions like speaking up or joining discussions are valuable. Third, the diary activity for Huckleberry Finn taught me perspective taking, but not in the way you might expect. In the original story, Pap Finn is simply an abusive drunk who never shows regret for his actions. However, when I wrote my diary entry, I made a creative choice to give him a moment of guilt and remorse. That act of writing as him forced me to step into the shoes of someone I would normally just hate. I had to ask myself, what kind of pain or weakness could lead a person to abuse their own child? And even though the real Pap Finn never apologizes, my version of him did, and that exercise helped me realize that people who do bad things are often broken themselves. Regret does not erase harm, but imagining it can help us understand why evil exists, and that understanding is the first step toward breaking the cycle of cruelty.

Personal Reflection

Looking back at this term so far, I realize that learning does not always have to be fun in a loud or playful way to be meaningful. At first, I was disappointed that we did not have roleplays or tableau like we did during prelims and midterms. Those activities made me feel excited and engaged, and I thought that without them, the lessons would be boring. But I was wrong. Even without the performances, the quiet moments of studying alone in my room on the evening of April 22, the deep class discussions about Romanticism and Realism, and even the serious, heavy themes like fighting for justice in Germinal or trying to understand an abusive father in Huckleberry Finn have taught me more about myself and the world than any skit ever could. The perfect score on my quiz made me happy, but I have realized that grades are not the most important thing. What matters more is how the lessons stay with you after the quiz is over. Wordsworth's poem stayed with me because I actually felt it. I have been that lonely person wandering around, feeling disconnected, and then finding peace in something as simple as a patch of flowers or the way the light hits the leaves. That is not just literature, that is life. Zola's Germinal stayed with me because it made me angry in a productive way. It made me think about my country and my responsibility as a citizen. It made me realize that being silent when things are wrong is its own form of complicity. I do not have to be a revolutionary leader, but I can start by caring, by paying attention, and by not laughing off injustices as just the way things are. The diary activity for Huckleberry Finn stayed with me the most, I think, because it asked me to be creative and empathetic at the same time. Choosing Pap Finn was a risk because he is such an unlikable character. But that was exactly why I picked him. I wanted to see if I could find something human in someone so terrible. In my diary, I wrote him as a man who drank to forget his own failures, who took out his anger on Huck because Huck was the only person weaker than him, and who, in a rare moment of sobriety and loneliness, finally saw what he had done and felt the weight of it. That was my imagination, not Mark Twain's. But the exercise taught me that empathy is a muscle. You have to use it even on people who do not deserve it, not to excuse them, but to understand how cruelty works. Because if you cannot understand the roots of evil, you cannot prevent it from growing again. Moving forward, I want to keep that balance in my life. I want to study hard and get good grades, but I also want to let myself feel deeply, to imagine freely, and to fight quietly for what I believe in. Whether through words, art, or simply refusing to stay silent when something is wrong, I want to be the kind of person who grows from every lesson, not just the fun ones. This term has been different, but different does not mean worse. Sometimes different means deeper. And I think I am learning to be okay with that.
