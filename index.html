Not So Big, Not So Bad (Reimagining Little Red Riding Hood)

January 29, 2026

Situation or Experience

We are so thankful kay Ms. Julia kase inadjust niya yung time ng performance namin sa Great Books. Ang napunta samin ay Little Red Riding Hood, maayos naman ang naging ka group ko, sina Ericka, Justin, Moises, Lee Laurence. Lahat kami ay talagang ginawa ang best para sa role play na iyon. From planning up until sa execution. Kahit na we faced so many challenges such as too many activities from other courses, props, to Sean Malaque who did not attend to that day, so we have to adjust everthing, because he will play the role of Hunter. So Justin and I decided to switch characters: Justin from Narrator to the Hunter, and me from Grandmother to Narrator. We are glad na pinuri ni Ms. Julia yung naging presentation namin Although we make a mistake, but those Mistake were not too big and not too bad. We thought she will going to criticize how and why we change role, but what we heard from her was purely a positive critique, also for all groups. That's why we are so happy. I also enjoy the performance of other groups. We are so shocked that we got perfect score in this presentation, it feels like lahat ng pagod, hirap, inis sa member kase minsan hindi nag paparticipate sa call at kung ano ano pang emosyon, lahat ng iyon e nawala HUHUHU kayaa sobrang thankful talaga kay Ms. Julia for appreciating our effort, also sa mga kagroup ko na ginawa rin yung best nila kahit wala kami talagang practice.

Emotional and Cognitive Response

I feel little nervous and mad after knowing that Sean will not be able to attend class. That time we have to think for alternative kase if we stick sa original plan, masisira yung flow, actually, due to a lack of members—especially girls—we decided to change the mother into a father. but the original story still visible. Instead na ipakita na kinakabahan kami ay we just remain calm, we are thankful sa time na ibinigay ni Ms. Julia for preparation, ginamit namin yun to modify the original plan. After the presentation we feel relief kase maayos naman na naexecute yung presentation namin. We are so happy and amuse the way Ms. Balag critique each group, because it is on point and we can really see that she is satisfied with all of the groups. We also take down notes the improvement she said, para next time mas maganda pa ang maging presentation.

SEL Insight

This situation shows social awareness and Responsible Decision making. When we carefully plan our modification to our existing flow, Actually hirap kami makaisip kung paano gagawin doon e, kase nga all goods na sana if pumasok lang si Sean. Justin as Narrator and Hunter goods na naman, kaso nakita namin sa script na magkasunod yung line ng Hunter and Narrator, so sabi ni Justin nakakalito siya if execution na, kaya napag isipan namin na maging Narrator na lang din ako, para maayos lang talaga yung flow nong kwento, at di rin malito yung mga manonood, di rin naman kase ako kasing galing ni Dave na kayang magpalit palit ng Character per line. In terms of social awareness, Justin and I decided to remove Sean to the group because he did not participate and contribute to the group, to be fair to all. Also Sean apologize to us that he was unable to attend class.

Personal Reflection

Always have a backup plan, since not every initial plan will work—just like what happened to us. Never let pressure eat you up, because if it does, everything will be destroyed. I am so thankful that I have this kind of team, where everyone gives their best to improve our performance. I also forgave Sean when he apologized, even though we had already given him a sanction. I felt bad about being mad at him, especially since it wasn’t because of a mean team or simply not wanting to attend class—he had his own reasons. So what I needed to do was to understand him. However, to be fair to all the members who contributed, Justin and I decided to remove his name from the list. Nakakapagod kase ang dami talaga naming need asikasuhin and gawin, but with Ms reaction and comment nakakalimutan namin yung pagod, and mapapasabi na lang talaga kami na worth it lahat. This situation helped me improve as someone who takes the initiative to establish a plan and make tough decisions. It also taught me to always look for the brighter side of every bad situation.

The Frozen Frame of a Filipino Bravery

March 5, 2026

Situation or Experiences

This is the day where in we need to present our Tableau or Frozen Scene activity about the themes in classical literature. Ang pinili ng grupo namin ay ang tema ng Bravery vs. Cowardice but we also want to incorparate the other themes as well to our scene. Ang naging grupo ko ay sina Ericka, Joebhert, Laurente, Taraya at Capitan. Bawat isa sa amin ay nag-isip kung paano namin maipapakita ang temang ito sa isang static scene. From planning the concept hanggang sa execution mismo ng tableau, we gave our best. We wanted to show the bravery of the Filipino people fighting for justice against a government that is not serving the country well. We explained it clearly: the leader in our scene, more concerned with money than his duty, also represented the "Sin" of greed and the cowardice of selfishness. Meanwhile, the people deciding to fight embodied "Virtue" and "Charity"—not just fighting for themselves, but helping the country and waking others up to the truth. Those who chose to ignore the situation represented selfishness. Nagulat kami sa galing ng ibang groups, lalo na nila Dave—sobrang angas at talagang pinag-isipan ang mga eksena. We were just thankful na kahit kami, na-execute namin nang maayos ang aming scene at naintindihan naman ng audience ang mensahe namin.

Emotional and Cognitive Response

Before our presentation, medyo kaba at pressure ang naramdaman ko. Ang bigat kasi ng tema, it's about our country and the fight for what is right. We wanted to do it justice. But instead of letting the pressure get to us, we focused on our plan. We visualized how we would stand frozen, what our facial expressions should convey, and how we would explain the symbolism of bravery and cowardice in our scene. After we presented, sobrang gaan ng pakiramdam ko. We felt relief and pride because we were able to clearly show our message about fighting for justice, even in a silent, frozen scene. I also really enjoyed watching the other groups; you could see the effort they put into portraying their themes. We also appreciate how Ms. Julia critique our Tableau sobrang ganda and gagaling daw ng lahat, nakita niya ang effort ng lahat at kami nakita rin namin ang effort ng bawat isa, kahit na medyo kulang sa time since may preparation pa for TNT ay talaga namang hinasayan at binigyang pansin ni Ms.

SEL Insight

This situation highlights Social Awareness and Responsible Decision-Making. We had to be socially aware to choose a theme that was not just from a book, but relevant to what is happening in our society today. We wanted to show the reality of corruption and the people's cry for true service. In terms of responsible decision-making, we carefully planned how to depict these complex ideas in a single, powerful frozen scene. We made sure that our body language, positions, and expressions clearly communicated the contrast between the corrupt leader and the brave citizens. We decided as a group to use our voices not through dialogue, but through the strength of our silent storytelling. Actually, we are thinking about whether we should continue with the Filipino Bravery content, since some of the group are leaning toward a Classical vibe, but we push to reflect the classical theme into the modern realities.

Personal Reflection

This activity taught me that a strong message doesn't always need words. A single, well-thought-out image, like our frozen scene, can speak volumes about bravery and the fight for what is right. We proved that you don't need elaborate props or lines to show the contrast between a selfish leader and a people united for justice. I am so thankful for my groupmates who gave their best to portray this important theme. Seeing the effort of Dave and the other groups also inspired me to push my own creativity further. This experience reinforced in me the importance of using our voices, even in a silent performance to reflect on the world around us and to stand for the values we believe in, like bravery, justice, and charity.
